About Us

Welcome to the About Me page for our Dumply App! We are a team of developers and environmental enthusiasts who are passionate about using technology to address the growing problem of electronic waste.

Our app aims to help individuals and businesses predict their e-waste generation and find sustainable solutions to manage it. We understand that e-waste is a complex issue that requires a multifaceted approach, and our app is just one small step towards a more sustainable future.

We believe that technology has the power to drive change and make a positive impact on the world. By creating this app, we hope to empower individuals and businesses to take action towards reducing their e-waste footprint and promoting a circular economy.

Thank you for choosing our app and joining us in our mission to create a more sustainable future!

Why E-waste Segregation is Important

E-waste refers to electronic waste, including discarded electronics like old mobile phones, computers, televisions, and other electronic devices.

E-waste contains toxic materials like lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants, which can cause serious health and environmental problems if not handled properly.

Most e-waste is generated in developed countries but is often shipped to developing countries for disposal, where it is often treated in unsafe and unregulated ways, causing further harm to the environment and public health.

E-waste segregation is essential to ensure that valuable materials like copper, gold, and silver can be extracted and recycled.

Some data regarding E-waste

According to the United Nations, the world generated 53.6 million metric tons of electronic waste in 2019, and this number is expected to reach 74 million metric tons by 2030.

The United Nations estimated that only 17.4% of the global e-waste generated in 2019 was collected and recycled, highlighting a significant gap in e-waste management practices.

E-waste segregation and recycling can provide a significant economic opportunity, with the recovery of materials like gold, silver, copper, and palladium estimated to be worth up to USD 21 billion globally.

That's Why:

E-waste segregation is not just an environmental issue but also a social and economic one, as it affects the health and livelihoods of people working in the informal e-waste sector and the availability of valuable materials for future use.
